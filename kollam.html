KOLLAM

**Kollam**, historically known as Quilon, is a coastal city in Kerala known for its rich trading history, scenic backwaters, and cultural heritage. It is one of the oldest ports on the Arabian Sea and was once a major center of international spice trade. Kollam is famous for the **Ashtamudi Lake**, the gateway to Kerala's backwaters, offering serene boat cruises and picturesque landscapes. The district is also known for its coir industry, cashew processing, and traditional art forms. With historic sites like **Thangassery Lighthouse**, **Kollam Beach**, and numerous temples and churches, Kollam blends natural beauty with a deep sense of history and spirituality, making it a unique destination in Kerala.
